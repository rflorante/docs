üìö Scrum.org Framework Alignment

  • Product Owner (PO): Manages product backlog, creates PRDs on Product Days, ensures value delivery through prioritization
  • Scrum Master (SM): Facilitates all ceremonies, coaches team on Scrum practices, removes impediments, protects team from disruptions
  • Development Team: Self-organizing unit including TL, FE, BE, QA, and UI/UX - collectively responsible for the Increment
  • Sprint Planning: PO presents priorities, team selects items and defines Sprint Goal with technical feasibility from TL
  • Daily Scrum: 15-minute sync for Development Team only - PM/SM may observe but not participate
  • Sprint Review: Demonstrate completed features, gather stakeholder feedback, adapt Product Backlog
  • Sprint Retrospective: Team inspects processes and creates improvement action items for next sprint

üéØ Product Day Optimization Strategy

  • Tuesday Focus: PO develops comprehensive PRDs, collaborates with UI/UX on mockups and user flows
  • Wednesday Gates: CTO/CPO review ensures technical feasibility and business alignment before backlog entry
  • Continuous Pipeline: While dev team works Mon/Thu-Sun, PO prepares future sprints on Tue/Wed
  • Stakeholder Alignment: Product days enable customer interviews and market research without disrupting development
  • Design Integration: UI/UX runs design sprints and validates concepts before development begins
  • Backlog Health: Maintains 2-3 sprints of refined, approved items ready at all times
  • Executive Visibility: Regular CTO/CPO touchpoints ensure strategic alignment and resource allocation
